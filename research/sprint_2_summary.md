### Notable Findings

**Scale Surprises:**
1. **Marsh Propane** (Mountain City, TN) — Far larger than expected: 69 employees, 16,000 customers, 7 locations, Top 50 national. Listed as 2-loc in dataset but actually has 7.
2. **Parker Oil/BT Energy** — Corporate subsidiary of Parker Oil (VA), not an independent. 30+ employees.
3. **Bumgarner Oil** — $26M total enterprise with 14 c-stores; propane is new (2021) segment.
4. **North State Gas** — Founded 1938, $5.5M revenue, Byers family 4th generation.

**M&A Intelligence:**
5. **Acme Propane Gas** — CONFIRMED acquired by Neill Gas (Sep 29, 2025). No longer independent target.
6. **Ormond Energy** — Active shareholder litigation (Worley v. Ormond) over contested $3M acquisition of New Hope Gas Co. Potential forced sale catalyst.
7. **Evergreen Propane** — Founded via acquisition of Wilson Farms Propane (2012). Platform acquisition model.
8. **Folger Gas** — Acquired Triple L Gas (2010); serves 12,000 customers with autogas school bus program.

**Succession Candidates:**
9. **Reed Oil** (Manteo, NC) — Founded 1932, no website, Outer Banks coastal monopoly. Classic succession candidate.
10. **Vaughan Gas** (Tuscumbia, AL) — 80 years old, no owner name publicly available. Low digital footprint.
11. **Pro Gas** (Dublin, GA) — ~98 years old, no website, no owner identifiable. Likely succession-motivated.

**Entity Clarifications:**
12. **Pico Propane** (Siler City, NC) — NOT the national Pico Propane and Fuels (TX). Separate locally-owned NC company (PFM Propane).
13. **Apex Propane** — Actually "Apex Gas & Appliance Co." — dual propane/appliance retail model.
